The World Cube Association (WCA) is the global organization that governs official Rubik's Cube Competitions, sets the rules, and maintains world records for twisty puzzles. It's the reason cubing is a real sport, not just a hobby.


What is the WCA?

The World Cube Association (WCA) is a non-profit organization that oversees competitive events for puzzles like the Rubik’s Cube and its many variants — 2×2, 4×4, Pyraminx, Skewb, and more. Founded in 2004 by Ron van Bruchem and Tyson Mao, the WCA was created to bring structure, fairness, and global recognition to the growing world of speedcubing.



What does the WCA Do?

Here's what the WCA is responsible for:

  • Organizing official competitions around the world — from local meets to world championships.
  • Standardizing rules for how puzzles are scrambled, timed, and judged.
  • Maintaining leaderboards and world records for all official events.
  • Training and certifying delegates who ensure competitions follow WCA regulations.
  • Promoting cubing as a sport, not just a passtime.

How Big is The WCA?

As of 2025, the WCA has hosted thousands of competiions across 100+ countries, with over 200,000 registered competitors. It's run by a board of directors, committees, and hundreds of certified delegates who ensure every competition meets global standards.


What Makes a Solve "Official"?

To be recongnized by the WCA:

  • The solve must happen at a WCA-sanctioned event.
  • The puzzle must be scrambled using official algorithms.
  • The timer must be a WCA-approved Stackmat timer.
  • The solve must be judged by a certified delegate.

Only then can your time be added to the global rankings.



Why does the WCA Matter?

Without the WCA:

  • There'd be no consistent rules.
  • World records wouldn't be verified.
  • Cubing wouldn't be a recognized sport.

With the WCA, cubing has become a global comepetitive scene — complete with ranking, rivalries, and championship titles.


Want to Compete?

You don't need to be a pro. Anyone can register for a WCA competition, get a WCA ID and start building their official statistics. It's the best way to meet other cubers, test your skills, and join the global cubing community.


You can learn more or find competitions near you at the official WCA website.
